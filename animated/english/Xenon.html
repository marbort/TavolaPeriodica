Xenon

Xenon is a noble gas (Z = 54) that was discovered in 1898 by the English chemists Sir William Ramsay and Morris William. Being a rare gas (0.000001% in the universe), its price per liter varies between 8 and 13 euros (from 11 to 15 dollars), or costs from 8000 to 13000 euros per cubic meter. The density of xenon is equal to 5.894 Kg / m 3 while that of the air is equal to 1.2 Kg / m 3 (in the picture it is shown that Xenon weighs more than air). At its solid state (at a temperature of -111.75 Â° C or 161.4 K) it has a cubic structure (a crystal is made up of 14 atoms as shown in the picture). Xenon is mainly used for white-blue light lamps (see photo), x-rays and, in the automotive industry, in car headlights and more recently as an "environmentally friendly" anesthetic that is easily disposable by the body (see photo).
