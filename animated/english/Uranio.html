Uranium

Uranium (U) has atomic number 92; it is a radioactive and toxic metal. In nature it is found in pure white-silver metallic form. It is found in the periodic table in the series of actinides. It has different isotopes including 234, 235 and 238, all radioactive. The most common is the isotope 238 U. The isotope 235 U, shown in yellow to highlight its radioactivity, is used as fuel in nuclear reactors, where nuclear fission takes place (in the picture). Nuclear fission is used to produce energy (see yellow flows and diffused light emanated from uranium) and consists in bombarding uranium with a neutron. The products of the nuclear reaction are neutrons, krypton and barium.
