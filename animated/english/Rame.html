Copper

Copper is a metal of the same group of gold and silver, sharing several physico-chemical properties with these elements. First known uses of copper trace back to 5000 BC and after 3000 BC it was added with tin to produce bronze. It is the second element in terms of thermal and electrical conductivity (after silver); these properties, together with its high ductility and malleability, make it a common occurence in many fields in pure form (e.g. it is used since 1877 to produce electrical cables and it is present in astronauts' suits to maintain body heat) or in alloys(more than 400 alloys contain copper). It is resistant to corrosion thanks to a natural film that forms on its surface and it can be recycled several times without losing its properties. Due to its antibacterical and antiseptical properties it is employed in coins, kitchenware and pipes.
