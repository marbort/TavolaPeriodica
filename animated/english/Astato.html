Astatine

Astatine is the element with atomic number 85: it is the heaviest halogen in nature. its name comes from the Greek term àstatos , which means unstable. Astatine is spontaneously produced from the radioactive decay of Thorium and Uranium, but even its most abundant isotope As-210 has a half life of approximately 8 hours, which is why Astatine is one of the most unstable elements, second only to Francium. It is extimated that the earth crust only contains 30 g of this element, so most of its characteristics remain unknown. It was first discovered in 1940 by Dale R. Corson, K. R. Mackenzie and E. Segrè when shooting Bismuth with alpha particles.
